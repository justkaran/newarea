Individual tutoring with AI

Computers have been our helpers for decades. But so far they have only been able to do exactly what we have told them to do beforehand. That is changing now: In Robo, our smart AI, CodeDoor learners find an almost human sparring partner.

Our Robo amazes CodeDoor users every day. As an individual tutor, he stands by the learners and helps when they get stuck.

Robo does not tell learners the solution, but supports the process of finding the right answer. He is always friendly and encouraging.

Organisations & Students Stories.

You have to have tried it yourself to imagine the easy and fast progress of CodeDoor learners. That's why we asked some alumni to tell us about their experiences.
Manal
Salesforce-Administrator

Manal her husband and three small children reached Germany by bus, rubber dinghy, boat, taxi and on foot. But Manal did not lose hope: she wanted to live independently and self-determined. As a CodeDoor learner, she stood out for her particular diligence. After an intensive training phase, she landed a job as an IT administrator. Through a partner organisation, she further qualified herself in parallel. For a few months now, she has been working permanently as a Salesforce administrator and is thus able to provide for her family safely!

Nagham
Coder to be

Nagham wanted to work for a bank. But due to her unconventional CV, she couldn't find an apprenticeship. Through the organisation that helped her, she came to CodeDoor and was thrilled: "I didn't even know that programming languages existed! I had never heard of them before," Nagham says today. She built a few projects and was able to start an apprenticeship as an application developer with this prior knowledge. "It's quite exhausting right now, I have to learn a lot. But if you want to achieve something, you have to make an effort. And it's worth it!"

Julia
Alumna and Tutor

Julia started as a learner with no previous coding experience, but with a background in design and a passion for languages. She dived into learning, starting with HTML, CSS and JS. She soon passed on her enthusiasm for Git to other students. Six months after Julia had written her first code, she got a job offer as a junior frontend developer from one of our most established CodeDoor tutors. Since then, they have been working side by side - both in the office and as CodeDoor tutors.

Milad
Front-end Developer

Milad has always loved sports. School, on the other hand, he loved less. Accordingly, it became difficult for him when he couldn't become a professional athlete - because he had hardly any other opportunities. With CodeDoor, he realised that the many years of consistent sports training eventually helped him: He impressed with his great ambition and consistency. He quickly qualified developed skills in front-end development, went to Switzerland and set up his own business there. Today he is a well booked freelancer and very satisfied: 'I am getting more experienced every day and can show more and more projects.'

Irina
Android developer in training

Irina participated in a 12-week design thinking program and successfully designed an app. She wanted to make her idea reality and just couldn't find an IT professional available to build her design. So Irina took her fate into her own hands and has been qualifying as an Android developer. 'The retraining is totally exhausting, but I haven't regretted my decision for a second yet.'

Ringaile
Blockchain Expert and Tutor

With a Masters in Computer Science in her pocket, Ringaile came to Germany for her first job. She is an IT all-rounder who absorbs new knowledge and seizes every opportunity to further her education. Multilingual, always friendly and well-balanced, she is a very popular tutor, especially for the more reserved learners. And while Ringaile combined a full-time job as a fullstack developer with her impressive sports program last year, she gained a qualification as blockchain expert!
